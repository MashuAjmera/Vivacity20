Rostrum

Description

Judging Criteria
• The oratory fluency of the contestants.
• The accuracy and relevance of the content.
• The relevance of the questions and answers asked in the Q&A round.
• The rebuttal given to the question posed by the other team.
• The conviction and confidence shown by the participants.

Rostrum Rules
1. The teams would consist of two participants.
2. The debate will be conducted in the form of knockout rounds:
Each team will be given a serial number and can challenge any one of the remaining teams. The challengee has to pick out a chit which would consist of three motions and choose one of them for the debate. The debate master decides which team stands in favour of the motion and which team opposes it.
3. A document will be provided to the teams containing the 10 topics.
4. Opening statement:
In the first stage, a team member from the teams paired against each other will present their opening statement. (Max. Duration – 3 min).
5. The second speaker of each team builds on the stance of their team while countering the opening statement of the opponent. (Max Duration – 2 min).
6. Question and Answer: Next stage will be a Question & Answer round where the teams will pose a question to the opponent. In this round, the one who presented the rebuttal will be answering the question posed by their opponent. No cross questioning will be allowed in this round. (Max. Duration – 1 min)
7. Closing Statement: Finally, the teams summarize their arguments and finish with a concluding statement. (Max. Duration – 1 min)
8. In the last two rounds where only the final four teams are left, the Debate Master shall provide both the teams with the agenda and their stance.

Just A Minute

Description

A fun form of verbal battle, wherein you compete as to who can speak the most in a creative way (without getting your mic seized).

1 FIRST ROUND
• The contestants will be divided into several groups.
• Each group will be given a random topic by the moderators upon which any person among the team can start with the discussion.
• Each group member can speak for a maximum of 1 minute. The moderator shall give a warning after 50 secs, followed by the final warning at the end of 60 secs.
• Anyone from the group can interject the speaker on grounds of 3 types of abnormalities, that are- Repetition, Stutter, and Deviation.
• The interjector has to carry on the discussion from the point where he/she interjected the speaker and complete their "JAM".
• After a speaker rests his/her voice after 60 secs, any group member can start another "JAM" until all of them have spoken.
• Marks shall be awarded on the basis of fluency, confidence, type of content, creativity and the impact on the audience.
• Additional marks will be given for the amount of time you speak and the number of interjections you make.

2 SECOND ROUND
• This round is a simple one. It will be a rapid-fire round in which the contestants will have to answer the questions asked by the moderator. The one who’ll answer the maximum number of questions in 1 minute will be crowned as Undisputed Champion of JAM - Just A Minute.
• But since every movie has a climax in it, there is one in this round too. The questions that will be asked by the moderator, the participants must answer them in an irrelevant way, failing to do so will lead to no further questions.

• For Example:
Moderator: What is your name?
You: I like Your shoes.

3 JUDGING CRITERIA
All the participants will be judged on the basis of the following factors: Creativity, Content, Fluency, Confidence, Impact on the audience.
In the first round, Judges will play their role by giving you marks on the following points:
• Repetition: if the speaker repeats any of his/her points in the span of 60 minutes.
• Stutter: If the speaker stutters or finds it difficult to speak about the topic in the span of 60 secs.
• Deviation: If the speaker deviates from the given topic or speaks about something non- relevant.
In Round 2 will be, you’ll be judged on the basis of the following:
• The maximum number of questions answered by one team/individual.
• How irrelevant and amusing their answer is!
